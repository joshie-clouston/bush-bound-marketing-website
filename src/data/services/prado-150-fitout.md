## What's included

The Prado 150 cargo area is roughly 1,000mm deep (seats up) or 1,830mm with the third row folded, about 1,100mm wide between the wheel arches, and 850mm tall to the cargo cover. It's not a huge space, but a well-designed drawer system transforms it from a jumbled mess into something genuinely organised.

Our Prado builds typically include a twin-drawer system with a fridge slide on one side, sized for common 12V fridges (36-50L). Above the drawers, we build a flat platform that works as a gear shelf or packing surface. For touring setups, we add a sleeping platform that extends with the tailgate open, plus a 12V system with a second battery and management system.

Everything is designed around keeping the third-row seats functional if you need them. The drawer system sits above the seat-fold line, so you can pull the build and have a seven-seater again in under 30 minutes.

## Why the Prado 150

The Prado 150 is one of the most capable and comfortable touring vehicles in Australia. Full-time 4WD, rear diff lock, and a suspension setup that handles corrugated roads all day without shaking your fillings out. It's also a great daily driver, which is why so many families run them.

Our fit-outs turn the Prado from a family SUV into a proper touring rig without sacrificing the things that make it practical during the week. The CNC-cut plywood fits precisely into the cargo area and looks like it belongs there.
